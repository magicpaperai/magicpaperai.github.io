A better way to work with LLMs

What is it?

LLMs, such as GPT3, unlock a whole new range of computer reasoning. Gone are the days when talking to a computer meant learning bash, Ruby, or JavaScript. Now all of a sudden computers speak the same languages we do.

Magic Paper is the best place to query LLMs, because it provides version control, forking, and social features on top of GPT3 prompts and responses. In short, Magic Paper brings all the functionality programmers have come to expect in their coding workflows to the world of LLM prompt-writing.

How does it work?

Get up and running in seconds by forking an existing prompt or quickly draft your own. Click "Run" at any point to append a GPT3 completion to your document. As you make edits, a full version history of the document will be saved automatically.

What else does it do?

As you browse Magic Paper, you'll find yourself copying and pasting sections from the prompts other people post. When you do, it'll be reflected in the version history of your document with traceback to the original. This is truly zero friction forking. And it's perfect for the spirit of remixing and cross-pollination that's inherent to all forms of writing, prompt-engineering as much as any.

Each document has two numbers printed next to it. Stars count the number of users who have starred a document as one of their favorites. Stripes count the number of documents that have included part of this one—either through copy-and-paste as mentioned above, or through forking.
